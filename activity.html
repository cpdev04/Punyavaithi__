Cancer Detection Camp For Women and Blood Donation Camp 2003

Cancer is a devastating disease that affects millions of people around the world. But with early detection, the chances of survival are significantly higher.

Blood Donation and Mega Health Camp 2004

In association with Punya Hospital, Naipunya conducted the camp. MP Smt Thejaswini Ramesh, was the chief guest of the event, along with local leaders of all parties. Our professors, teachers, and staff took an active part in the camp that provides efficient results to several needy people.

Cultural and Sports Meet 2005

The Naipunya foundation's latest event was a smashing success, as they organized a cultural and sports meet for children. The event was full of energy and enthusiasm, as the children showcased their talents in a variety of sports and cultural activities. The foundation's efforts in organizing this event have made a lasting impact on the children.

Initiated Laparoscopic Learning 2006

Our Naipunya foundation is leading the way in laparoscopic surgery education, empowering surgeons to provide safer, less invasive procedures for their patients. We initiated laparoscopic learning with the help of teachers and friends in early 2006.

We Opened New Clinic In The Year 2007

Our new clinic is blessed by a spiritual leader. We're thrilled to announce that Sri Sri Sri Balagandahranatha Swamiji has blessed our new clinic. With his divine guidance, we're excited to continue providing quality healthcare to those in need.

Improving the lives of underprivileged children

Naipunya Foundation is a non-profit organization dedicated to improving the lives of underprivileged children in India. One of the foundation's key initiatives is its "Education for All" program, which seeks to provide access to education for children who may not otherwise have the means to attend school.

Free Health Camp At Vidyananda Nagara

Naipunya Foundation, in association with Punya Hospital, organized a free health check-up camp for underprivileged communities in the region.
